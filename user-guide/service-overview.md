## Who the service is for

The Australian Nextflow Seqera Service supports Australian life science researchers who use Nextflow workflows and need a central interface for managing, launching and monitoring pipeline execution.

The service supports users with different levels of technical expertise. Researchers can use an existing workspace and preconfigured compute environment, while workspace administrators and technical staff can manage participants, credentials and infrastructure connections.

## What the service provides

- Access to the Seqera Platform through an Australian national service
- Shared organisation workspaces
- Management of workflows, launch configurations and runs
- Integration with supported HPC, cloud and other compute environments
- Central monitoring, logs and run metadata
- Guidance and support for using the Australian service

## What users remain responsible for

Depending on your workspace and compute provider, users or their organisation may remain responsible for obtaining infrastructure access, maintaining allocations and storage permissions, selecting scientifically appropriate workflow settings, interpreting outputs, and meeting institutional data-security requirements.

## User roles

### Workspace participant

A participant can access resources made available in a workspace. Available actions depend on the role assigned by the workspace administrator.

### Workspace administrator

An administrator manages participants and may configure shared workflows, credentials and compute environments.

### Infrastructure or platform specialist

A specialist may configure HPC agents, cloud permissions, queues, storage paths and other technical settings on behalf of a workspace.
